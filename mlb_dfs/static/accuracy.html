What these numbers mean

Bias is the average miss (actual minus projected). A bias near 0 means we're not systematically too high or too low — the single hardest thing to get right, and where most models quietly drift. MAE is the typical size of a single-game miss; baseball is high-variance, so a hitter MAE around 4 fantasy points is genuinely tight.

We don't hand-tune to look good. Every change is A/B-replayed against real outcomes before it ships, and rejected if it doesn't hold up. That discipline is the product.
